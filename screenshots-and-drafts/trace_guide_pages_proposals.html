Guide / Typography / Lee’s Palace
Lee’s Palace
Typography ⚲ Bloor St W, The Annex 80m away
About this gem

A beloved Toronto music venue since 1958, Lee’s Palace is as famous for its hand-painted exterior mural as for the bands that have played inside. The facade—featuring a wildly detailed cartoon cityscape in primary colours—is one of the most photographed pieces of signage in the city.

Style
Hand-lettered
Mural signage
Pop vernacular
1950s throwback
Designer’s note
The cartoon-style lettering on the facade breaks every rule of Swiss typography—and that’s exactly what makes it memorable. Notice how the thick-to-thin stroke variation mimics hand-painted circus lettering from a century earlier.
